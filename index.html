Recommended format

Instagram Reel cover size guide

A vertical 9:16 image works best for Reel covers. A common high-quality size is 1080 × 1920 pixels.

Keep your important text, face, and logo near the center area. Top and bottom areas can be affected by app interface elements, captions, or preview crops.

Quick recommendation

  • Use vertical 9:16 format
  • Prefer 1080 × 1920 px
  • Keep text inside the safe zone
  • Check profile grid crop before posting
  • Avoid placing logos at extreme edges

Placement matters

Safe zone guide

ReelCoverFit highlights practical unsafe areas so you can avoid placing important elements too close to the edges.

Top area

Avoid placing important text very close to the top because it may feel cramped in previews.

Bottom area

Captions, buttons, or interface elements can make bottom text harder to read.

Center area

The center is usually safest for faces, titles, and logos.

Check Instagram Reel Cover Size Before Posting

ReelCoverFit is a free Instagram Reel size checker built for creators, students, editors, small business owners, and anyone who wants their Reel cover to look clean before posting. Many creators design a good cover image, add title text, place a face or logo, and then later notice that the cover looks cropped, misaligned, or unreadable in different Instagram previews. This tool helps you check your Reel cover size, safe zone, and profile grid crop in one simple place.

The recommended format for a Reel cover is a vertical 9:16 layout. A common high-quality size is 1080 × 1920 pixels. If your cover image is too wide, too narrow, or not close to the 9:16 ratio, important parts of your design may get cropped when it appears as a Reel thumbnail. ReelCoverFit shows your uploaded image inside a vertical preview so you can quickly understand whether your Instagram Reel cover size is suitable or needs adjustment.

The most useful part of this tool is the Reel cover safe zone preview. A cover may look perfect while editing, but text placed too close to the top, bottom, or edges can become harder to read after posting. ReelCoverFit highlights safe and unsafe areas so you can keep important elements like titles, faces, product photos, logos, and call-to-action text near the center. This helps reduce the chance of your Reel cover text getting cut or hidden.

ReelCoverFit also includes a profile grid crop preview. Instagram content can appear differently in the Reel view, profile grid, and thumbnail preview. A cover that looks good in full vertical size may not look as good when it appears as a square grid crop on your profile. With the grid preview, you can check whether the main subject of your cover stays visible in the center crop before you upload it to Instagram.

This Instagram Reel cover checker works directly inside your browser. You do not need to create an account, install an app, or upload your image to a server. When you select an image, the preview is created locally on your device. This makes the tool simple, fast, and privacy-friendly. You can upload a JPG, PNG, WebP, or other supported image file, check its dimensions, preview the safe zone, and download a checked preview for reference.

Use ReelCoverFit whenever you are designing a new Reel thumbnail, editing a promotional cover, creating a tutorial Reel, posting a college event video, or preparing content for a brand page.
