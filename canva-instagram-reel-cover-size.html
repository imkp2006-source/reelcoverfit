Canva workflow

Canva Instagram Reel Cover Size Workflow

If you design Reel covers in Canva, the safest workflow is to create a vertical 9:16 design, keep important elements near the center, export clearly, and test the cover before posting.

Check your cover before posting

Upload your image to ReelCoverFit and preview the 9:16 fit, safe zone, and profile grid crop.

Use Free Reel Cover Checker

Recommended Canva setup

  • Use a vertical 9:16 canvas.
  • Keep the title short and bold.
  • Place the face, product, or main subject near the center.
  • Avoid tiny text near the bottom.
  • Export as a clear JPG, PNG, or WebP image.

Why you should test after Canva export

A design may look good inside Canva but still feel cropped in a Reel preview or profile grid. ReelCoverFit helps you check whether the cover stays readable before you upload it to Instagram.

Step-by-step workflow

  1. Create a vertical Reel cover design in Canva.
  2. Add your title, face, logo, or product near the center.
  3. Export the cover image.
  4. Open ReelCoverFit.
  5. Upload the exported image.
  6. Check the safe zone and profile grid crop.
  7. Go back to Canva and adjust if needed.

Quick quality checklist

  • Use readable contrast between text and background.
  • Use fewer words on the cover.
  • Keep enough padding around the main title.
  • Check the profile grid crop before publishing.
Tip: If the design looks busy in the grid preview, simplify the cover before posting.
